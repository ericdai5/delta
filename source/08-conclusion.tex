\section{Conclusion}

This paper presents Delta, a DSL for adding interactivity to typeset formulas in web-based authoring environments. In our studies, Delta reduced the time and effort of editing a formula augmentation, and was picked up and used by users to devise their own augmentations from scratch. Language constructs were largely described as intuitive and we regularly saw their successful use. Our own explorations have shown Delta capable of producing rich augmented formulas that employ patterns of in-situ calculation, walkthrough explanations, graphical notation, and linked visuals. Altogether, this paper develops and refines an important kind of tool for realizing the expressive potential of interactive science texts.
